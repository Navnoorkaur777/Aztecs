Yatra/Jatra


It is traditional folk theatre of eastern India. The origin of this musical drama or theatre intrinsically a musical theatre form, is traditionally credited to the rise of Sri Chaitanya's Bhakti movement,

Swang


It is very famous traditional theatre in the state of Punjab, Haryana and Odisha. It was performed in rural area of Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, Uttrakhand and Bengal. The softness of emotions, sharpness of dialogue and specific costumes are some features of this theatre.

Nautanki


It is a famous traditional theatre of Northern India. It is said that this style was developed from 'Bhagat' form of theatre which is approximately 400 years old, while the word 'Nautanki' come into existence only in 19th century.

Dashavatar


It is the most developed and famous theatre of Konkan and Goa region. The performers personify the 10 incarnations of Lord Vishnu and performed during religious ceremonies.

Kariyala


t is Hindustani folk-dance drama or theatre of Rajasthan. They are performed exclusively by men, are characterized by the powerful body movements of the performers, and include mime and chanting.
